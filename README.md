🚢 ORCA — Objective Risk & Capital Allocation System

🧠 WHAT IS ORCA?
ORCA is a systematic equity analysis framework designed to identify high-probability 
entry points in high-quality companies based on Intrinsic Value, Asset Quality, 
and Disciplined Capital Allocation.

It does not predict market bottoms.
It identifies zones of asymmetric risk/reward.

🎯 OBJECTIVES
- Avoid premature entries ("falling knives").
- Allocate capital only when risk/reward is favorable.
- Enforce discipline by removing emotional decision-making.
- Tiered Allocation: Limit position sizing based on fundamental strength.

⚙️ CORE COMPONENTS

1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
Estimates intrinsic value based on future cash flows using a conservative 
discount rate and capped growth projections.

2. Mean Reversion (MR)
Anchors valuation to historical and sector norms using a weighted average 
of company and industry multiples (70/30 split).

3. Quality Score (QS)
Proprietary 0-100 score evaluating:
- Profitability (ROE, Margins)
- Financial Strength (Current Ratio, Debt-to-Equity)
- Growth (Revenue Growth)

------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------

🧭 PHILOSOPHY
“I choose the companies. ORCA chooses the price and the weight.”

ORCA separates:
- Selection → Investor (Quality businesses)
- Execution → System (Timing, Discipline & Sizing)

📊 STRATEGY RULES
- Only buy when ORCA = BUY.
- Never buy on HOLD or SELL.
- Avoid overriding signals with intuition.
- Never exceed the Max Weight dictated by the asset's Tier.
